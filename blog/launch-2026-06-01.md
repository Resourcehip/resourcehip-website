## Resourcehip is open for ratings

For the last few months we have been quiet, building the HIP methodology — a seven-dimension rating that asks how a consumer product holds up against material scarcity, supply-chain risk, recyclability, repairability, social and environmental impact, longevity, and a Regenerative Index that runs from −10 to +10. Today we are open for ratings.

Three things to look at first.

**An air fryer**, scored 2.9 out of 10 against the category baseline. The interesting number is the ceiling: 7.1. That is the gap between what we publish in the absence of evidence and what a real, well-made air fryer can score with its actual materials and supply chain on the table. → [HIP rating: Air Fryers](/ratings/air-fryers)

**A cork product**, scored 5.2 out of 10 with a Regenerative Index of 0 — Renewable. Cork is FSC-certified, sustainably harvested without cutting trees, and the warranty on a typical product runs ten years. The HIP score is held back by mixed fasteners and limited end-of-life options, not the material. The methodology rewards what the material does and penalises what the assembly does — separately. → [HIP rating: Cork Products](/ratings/cork-products)

**Wireless earbuds**, scored 1.8 out of 10. Critical raw materials (cobalt, neodymium, gold, lithium), single-jurisdiction extraction, glued housings, no spare parts, three-year design life. The lowest of the three. The ceiling, even on the best wireless earbuds we have seen described in evidence, sits at 5.9 — below the Standard HIP Mark threshold of 6.0. → [HIP rating: Wireless Earbuds](/ratings/wireless-earbuds)

Each rating publishes the score for every dimension, the rationale, the data sources, and the conservative assumptions made where evidence was not public. Methodology v1.3 is published in full at [resourcehip.com/methodology](/methodology/) under Creative Commons (CC BY-NC 4.0). It is meant to be read.

If you make a product and want it scored on its actual materials and supply chain rather than the conservative category baseline, we are now taking commissions: [resourcehip.com/pricing](/pricing). The first five customers to commission a paid rating before 30 September 2026 receive a 30% launch discount.
