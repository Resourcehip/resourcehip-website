# Pricing

Resourcehip publishes ratings in three forms. **Category Ratings** are free public assessments of whole product categories. **Generic Ratings** and **Verified Ratings** are paid, product-specific assessments commissioned by manufacturers — a Verified Rating is what licences the HIP Mark.

## Free: Category Ratings

Category Ratings cover whole product categories — for example, "kettles" or "cordless drills" — and use conservative defaults wherever real product data is not public. They are free for anyone to read and they are deliberately conservative. A real product, scored on its actual materials and supply chain, will almost always score higher than the category baseline.

There is no charge to be listed against a Category Rating. There is no opt-out either: if a category we publish includes your product, the conservative baseline applies.

## Paid: Generic and Verified Ratings

If you make the product, a Category Rating is not the end of the story. The conservative baseline is what we publish in the absence of evidence. The space between that baseline and your product's real performance is your headroom — and a paid rating turns it into a number you can show.

Two product-specific options:

| Rating | What it is | Price |
|---|---|---|
| **Generic Rating** | A product-specific HIP score scored from public data plus structured information you provide. No supplier evidence dossier required. Faster turnaround. Suitable for early benchmarking and internal use. | **£350** per rating |
| **Verified Rating** | A product-specific HIP score scored against manufacturer-submitted evidence (materials, supply chain, repairability, end-of-life). Reviewed by our assessors. **Required to licence the HIP Mark.** | **£500** per rating |

Both rating types deliver:

- A full assessment against all seven HIP dimensions
- A published rating page on resourcehip.com tied to your brand and product
- A written rationale for each dimension score, including the evidence used
- A category peer comparison so you can see how the rating sits against the free baseline

A Verified Rating additionally includes a 24-month HIP Mark licence (renewable annually after that at £75 per year per product) for products that score 6.0 or above.

### Annual subscriptions

If you plan to rate several products a year, a subscription is cheaper per rating and includes priority handling.

## The HIP Mark

A Verified Rating that scores 6.0 or above is eligible to licence the HIP Mark — the consumer-facing badge that signals an independently assessed product. The HIP Mark has three tiers: **Standard** (HIP ≥ 6.0), **Silver** (HIP ≥ 7.5), and **Gold** (HIP ≥ 9.0 with a Regenerative Index of +6.0 or above).

The HIP Mark licence is included in the Verified Rating fee for 24 months from the date the rating is published. After that the renewal fee is £75 per year per product. The licence is tied to a specific product and rating version; a substantively new formulation requires a new rating.

## Frequently asked questions

### What is the difference between a Category Rating and a Generic Rating?

A Category Rating is free, covers a whole category, and uses conservative defaults. A Generic Rating is paid (£350), covers one specific product from one named manufacturer, and uses the structured information you submit alongside public data — without requiring a full evidence dossier. A Generic Rating will almost always score higher than its Category baseline, because it is scored on the product, not the worst-case assumption for the category.

### What is the difference between a Generic Rating and a Verified Rating?

A Verified Rating (£500) is scored against a full evidence dossier — materials, supply-chain provenance, repairability, end-of-life — that our assessors review. A Verified Rating is the only rating that licences the HIP Mark. A Generic Rating is faster and cheaper but is not HIP-Mark eligible.

### How long does a rating take?

A Generic Rating: 5–7 working days from submission. A Verified Rating: 2–4 weeks from a complete submission. Most of that time on a Verified Rating is reviewing your evidence and following up on the supply chain; the scoring itself is fast.

### What data do I need to submit?

For a Generic Rating: a structured product information form (we send the template). For a Verified Rating: materials composition (down to component level for assemblies), supplier provenance for primary materials, repairability documentation, end-of-life pathway information, and supporting documents. We send the full submission template after your enquiry — you do not need to assemble everything upfront.

### What if I do not have all the data?

Send what you have. We will tell you what is missing and what is needed to produce a defensible score. In some cases a rating can proceed with documented assumptions on minor inputs; in others it cannot. We will be honest about which it is before you commit.

### Pay-per-rating or subscription — which should I pick?

If you expect to rate fewer than four products in the next year, pay-per-rating is simpler. If you expect four or more — or if you have a portfolio you want to benchmark over time — Annual Base is cheaper per rating. Annual Premium is for portfolios that include a mix of Generic and Verified ratings.

### How is the HIP Mark licensed?

The licence is included in the Verified Rating fee for 24 months from the date the rating is published. It permits use of the HIP Mark on the rated product's packaging, in product-specific marketing, and on the product's page on your website. The mark is tied to one product and one rating version; it does not extend to a brand or a wider range.

### What happens at renewal?

After 24 months we contact you with a renewal offer at £75 per year per product. You can renew on the existing rating, commission an updated rating, or let the licence lapse. If the licence lapses, the published rating remains live but the HIP Mark may no longer be displayed on the product or its marketing.

### What if a published rating is wrong?

You can dispute a rating at any time at no cost. The process is described on our [Dispute](/dispute) page. If new evidence changes a score, we update the published rating and note the revision.
