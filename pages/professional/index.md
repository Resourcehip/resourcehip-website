## Scoring Dimensions

The HIP Score (0-10) is composed of seven weighted dimensions:

1. **Material Criticality** — how abundant or at-risk are the raw materials?
2. **Supply Chain Transparency** — how traceable is the supply chain?
3. **Environmental Impact** — what is the environmental footprint of production?
4. **Repairability** — can the product be repaired and maintained?
5. **Recyclability** — can materials be recovered at end of life?
6. **Longevity** — how long is the product designed to last?
7. **Ethical Sourcing** — are materials sourced under fair and safe conditions?

## The Regenerative Index

Inside the HIP Score sits the Regenerative Index (RI) — a unique scale from -10 to +10 that distinguishes products depleting finite reserves from those actively rebuilding natural capital.

| Band | Range | Meaning |
|------|-------|---------|
| Depleting | -10 to -6 | Actively drawing down finite material reserves |
| Extractive | -5 to -1 | Net material consumer, limited recovery |
| Renewable | 0 | Material equilibrium — input matches recovery |
| Restorative | +1 to +5 | Net material restorer, partial regeneration |
| Regenerative | +6 to +10 | Actively rebuilding natural capital |

Most consumer products today sit below zero.

## For Retailers and Buyers

If you are a retailer, procurement officer, or buying team evaluating products for your range:

- **Category ratings** show the baseline material resilience of a product type — useful for benchmarking and category-level risk assessment
- **Verified ratings** for specific products provide independently assessed, citable evidence for procurement decisions
- **The HIP Mark** (Standard, Silver, Gold) provides a simple, consumer-facing signal that a product has met a verified threshold
- **Pricing and service terms** are published transparently — see our [schedule of fees](/pricing)
